Book Reviews

Critical perspectives on new scholarship, history, and strategy in the Indo-Pacific.

Ghost Fleet: A Novel of the Next World War

By P.W. Singer & August Cole  |  Houghton Mifflin Harcourt

A near-future thriller that has become required reading in military academies. Singer and Cole weave a scenario of great power conflict in the Pacific that highlights the vulnerabilities of high-tech systems and the continued relevance of geography. A crucial, if fictional, look at how a war in our region might actually unfold.

ISBN: 978-0544142848
Amazon Apple Books Google Books

Asian Waters: The Struggle Over the South China Sea

By Humphrey Hawksley  |  Duckworth

Hawksley provides a vivid, on-the-ground account of the maritime disputes defining the 21st century. While focused on the South China Sea, the analysis of gray-zone tactics and fishing militia operations offers direct parallels to the situation currently unfolding in Las Ladrones' northern waters.

ISBN: 978-1468316521
Amazon Apple Books Google Books

Destined for War: Can America and China Escape Thucydides's Trap?

By Graham Allison  |  Houghton Mifflin Harcourt

The defining text on the structural dangers of the US-China rivalry. Allison's historical case studies are essential for understanding the systemic pressures that place small states like Las Ladrones in the crossfire. Though sometimes overly deterministic, the framework remains indispensable for policymakers.

ISBN: 978-0544935273
Amazon Apple Books Google Books

The End of Grand Strategy

By Simon Reich and Peter Dombrowski  |  Cornell University Press

A provocative argument that the era of coherent, overarching grand strategy is over, replaced by ad-hoc calibration. For Ladronese strategists trying to predict American behavior, this book offers a sobering explanation for the inconsistencies often seen in Washington's Pacific policy.

ISBN: 978-1501714444
